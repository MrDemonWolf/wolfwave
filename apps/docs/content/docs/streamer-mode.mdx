<span className="docs-eyebrow">Guide</span>

Streamer Mode hides anything you would not want a viewer to read off your screen. Turn it on before you share your screen or show Settings on camera.

## What it masks

With Streamer Mode on, these are replaced with a `••••••` mask everywhere they appear, in Settings and in the menu bar:

- Your connected Twitch channel name
- The overlay and widget URLs
- The WebSocket server address
- The Stream Widgets access token

The **Copy** and **Open** buttons next to a masked value are switched off too, so a stray click cannot leak it into your clipboard or a browser.

## What it does not change

Streamer Mode only changes what is on screen. It does not touch how WolfWave works:

- Your overlay keeps receiving now-playing data.
- Discord Rich Presence keeps updating.
- Twitch chat output is unchanged.

So you can leave it on during a whole stream with no downside.

## Turn it on

Streamer Mode is a tray-menu toggle. Open the WolfWave menu bar icon and flip **Streamer Quick Actions → Streamer Mode**. It is a one-tap switch, on or off, and the setting is remembered.

## When to use it

- Screen-sharing your whole desktop.
- Walking through Settings on camera.
- Recording a tutorial or a bug report screenshot.

Pair it with [Back Up & Restore](/docs/backup) and [Stream Widgets](/docs/features) for a setup that is safe to show and easy to move between machines.
